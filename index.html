// 001 — About

The Physicist
Behind the Equations

I am a postdoctoral Research Associate at the Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar (IIT Gandhinagar), working at the intersection of quantum sensing, open quantum systems, and atomtronics.

I completed my Ph.D. at IISER Pune in December 2025, under the supervision of Prof. Rejish Nath, where my thesis focused on droplets and supersolids in dipolar Bose-Einstein condensates — a frontier where quantum fluctuations, long-range interactions, and emergent order intertwine in beautiful and surprising ways.

My work combines analytical methods with large-scale numerical simulations — Path Integral Quantum Monte Carlo, Gross-Pitaevskii imaginary/real-time evolution, and variational techniques — to uncover the rich physics of quantum matter, spanning both weakly and strongly correlated regimes.

// 002 — Research

Research Interests

Probing the quantum world through theory, simulation, and mathematical rigor.

🫧
Dipolar Bose-Einstein Condensates
Studying droplet arrays and supersolid phases in doubly-dipolar and multilayer dipolar BECs, combining long-range dipole-dipole interactions with quantum fluctuation effects.
🔷
Quantum Many-Body Physics
Developing and applying Path Integral Quantum Monte Carlo methods to uncover emergent order and phase transitions across weakly and strongly correlated quantum systems.
🌀
Rotating Quantum Gases
Investigating supersolidity in rotated dipolar BECs, exploring how rotation-induced vortices interact with density-wave order in exotic quantum phases.
🛰️
Quantum Sensing
Harnessing ultracold atomic systems for ultra-precise measurement and sensing, exploiting quantum coherence and entanglement for metrological advantages beyond the standard quantum limit.
⚛️
Open Quantum Systems
Studying decoherence, dissipation, and non-equilibrium dynamics of quantum systems coupled to their environment, bridging fundamental theory and practical quantum technologies.
🔁
Atomtronics
Engineering matter-wave circuits using ultracold atoms in tailored optical potentials — atomic analogues of electronic circuits for next-generation quantum devices.
